### 2-Minute Version
The thread that runs through my career is building and scaling — taking something from zero or near-zero and growing it into a high-performing system and team. I've done that as an engineer, as an entrepreneur, and as a leader.

I started at Amazon in 2014 as an SDE on AWS, working across RDS Aurora and Simple Workflow. SWF is where I fell in love with distributed systems. At the same time, I co-founded Medimap — working 80-hour weeks, evenings and weekends building a healthcare marketplace that reached 72% Canadian market share and a $1.5M seed round.

In 2018, my manager advocated for me to take over Step Functions as a first-time manager. I inherited a team of two, grew it to eight in five months, and scaled to 22 over four years. I was promoted three times — SDM II → SDM III → Senior Manager — and led the escalation manager group of 16 EMs across Events and Workflows.

In 2022, I deliberately moved to Alexa to broaden: from B2B to B2C, from infrastructure to product, and into AI and science. I also wanted to work again with a past director who I saw as an excellent product mentor. I found a very different operating culture — less rigor than AWS — so I brought what I knew. Within five months I delivered a 54.5% infrastructure cost reduction. Today I lead an organization of 61 across multiple geographies.

What I bring that's different is the combination: deep technical roots from distributed systems and a startup, plus leadership at scale — from 2 direct reports to an organization of 61 at Amazon.
